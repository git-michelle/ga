ABOUT US


We're Dedicated to Our Community


Gift Angels Foundation is a non-profit organization, with a focus on providing necessary services for the affected communities in Uganda. The organization was founded in March 2015 by four co-founders; Richard, Violet, Peter, and Michael, who are all currently involved in Gift Angels Foundation as active directors.

With their collective shared backgrounds in business administration, hospitality, concept development, global development issues, and sustainability, they are leading Gift Angels Foundation's initiatives towards building a stronger community within the regions of Uganda.

Our Vision

Community development is crucially needed in Uganda and we believe through educational efforts and direct community engagement, the communities will change positively.

We strive to provide the youths within the Ugandan communities access to basic education, developmental programs for generating income, and informational campaigns to raise awareness on overall health and sexual/reproductive health, especially for HIV-positive individuals.

Our Mission


At Gift Angels Foundation, we believe in utilizing all the tools at our disposal to contribute to community development over a singular focus on one specific development issue. This has allowed us to develop an ongoing and continuously updated list of objectives for our initiatives:

  • Reduce the level of illiteracy for the youth, specifically OVCs (Orphans & Vulnerable Children) throughout the regions of Uganda

  • Develop and provide awareness campaigns for improving overall health and HIV/AIDS prevention

  • Build and establish a youth center to keep youth off the streets and improve the communityâ€™s social environment

  • Develop and provide informational campaigns that empower the youth and women of Uganda to learn practical skills to improve their economic status

  • Provide OVCs with necessities, such as food, shelter, medical care, and access to education

  • Raise awareness of the needs of OVCs in Uganda and increase the general household standards of living with OVCs
